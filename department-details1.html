What We Track & Analyse

Market sizing is only useful if it reflects the opportunity available to your specific product, in your specific target segment, at your specific entry timing. We build market intelligence that accounts for all three — not the headline TAM that looks good in a pitch deck.

Fintech Funding Flow Analysis

We track African fintech funding by country, category, and stage — breaking down which markets are attracting capital, which product verticals are commanding premium valuations, and what investor appetite signals about near-term competitive intensity.

Remittance Corridor Mapping

The Africa–Europe remittance corridor moves billions annually. We map the dominant rails, the key players — Wise, Lemfi, Flutterwave, Paystack, WorldRemit — the fee structures, and where margin compression and white space still exist for new entrants with the right positioning.

Addressable Market Sizing

We size the realistic addressable market for your product category in each target market — separating the total market from the actual opportunity available given your licence type, pricing model, customer segment, and entry timing. TAM is a story. SAM is a decision tool.

Entry Timing Assessment

Market timing is as important as market size. We assess regulatory readiness, competitive saturation trajectory, infrastructure maturity, and consumer adoption curves to identify whether your entry window is opening, peaking, or closing — and what that means for your capital deployment strategy.

Investor Landscape Mapping

For operators raising alongside an expansion, we map the active investors in each corridor — Partech, TLcom, Flourish, QED, Norrsken22, Catalyst Fund — their portfolio overlaps, their typical cheque sizes, and their strategic preferences at Series A and B stage.

Unit Economics Benchmarking

We benchmark the unit economics of comparable operators in your target market — CAC, LTV, churn, take rate, and margin profile — so your financial model is calibrated against what's actually achievable, not what your spreadsheet assumes.

Why Market Intelligence Has to Precede the Business Case

The business case you present to your board is only as strong as the market assumptions underneath it. If those assumptions are built on outdated data, optimistic TAM figures, or a misread of competitive dynamics — the business case is structurally unsound before anyone has spent a dollar.
