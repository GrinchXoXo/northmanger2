What Our Competitive Intelligence Covers

Most competitive analyses describe who exists. Ours describes what they control, where they're vulnerable, and what that means for your specific entry strategy. The goal isn't a list of competitors — it's a clear picture of the competitive landscape you're entering and what position you can realistically occupy within it.

Category Saturation Heatmaps

We map the density of fintech players across every major product category — payments, lending, savings, remittance, B2B infrastructure, insurance, FX, and wealth — in each of our six core markets. Some categories are saturated. Some are not. You need to know which is which before you enter.

White Space Detection

We identify the underserved segments, distribution gaps, and product categories where meaningful entry opportunity still exists for well-capitalised operators with the right positioning. Not all white space is equal — we grade opportunity by accessibility, defensibility, and time horizon.

150+ Fintech Profile Database

Each profile includes: licensing status and jurisdiction, product vertical and feature set, fee structure and pricing model, settlement rails and banking partners, funding history and investor relationships, expansion history and current market footprint. Updated continuously.

Competitor Positioning Analysis

For specific entry scenarios, we produce detailed competitive positioning maps — showing where each incumbent is strong, where they are exposed, what their pricing floor and ceiling are, and how a new entrant can differentiate rather than compete head-on in their strongest segment.

Market Movement Tracking

The competitive landscape in these markets moves fast — new entrants, regulatory changes, funding rounds, and product pivots shift the map continuously. Our intelligence retainer clients receive ongoing updates so their competitive picture stays current as they execute their expansion.

Partnership & Rail Intelligence

In the EU–Africa corridor, the partnership stack — banking partners, settlement rails, distribution channels — is as strategically important as the product itself. We map who controls what infrastructure, who is exclusive, and where the open access points are for new market entrants.

Why Generic Competitor Research Isn't Enough

A Google search tells you who exists. It doesn't tell you who controls the distribution, who has the exclusive banking relationships, which categories are structurally crowded versus just noisily crowded, or where the consumer pain points remain genuinely unaddressed. That's the intelligence that determines whether your entry succeeds.
